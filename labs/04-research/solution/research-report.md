# Obsidian vs Notion (personal knowledge work)

## Facts

- F1: Obsidian publishes a free personal-use license and lists paid Sync and Publish add-ons. Source: https://obsidian.md/pricing
- F2: Notion publishes Free, Plus, and Business plans. Source: https://www.notion.so/pricing
- F3: Obsidian documents that notes are stored as local files in a vault. Source: https://help.obsidian.md/Files+and+folders/How+Obsidian+stores+data
- F4: Obsidian documents its Sync service separately from local storage. Source: https://help.obsidian.md/Obsidian+Sync/Introduction+to+Obsidian+Sync

## Inferences

- I1: For a solo worker who treats local files as a hard requirement, Obsidian aligns more directly with that requirement. Supports: F3
- I2: The migration decision should compare optional sync cost separately from the availability of a free personal-use license. Supports: F1, F2, F4

## Recommendations

- R1: Pilot Obsidian for two weeks with a representative 20-note subset before deciding whether to migrate the full archive. Supports: I1
- R2: Compare the paid sync option with a self-managed backup workflow before treating the free personal-use license as the total operating cost. Supports: I2

## Unknowns

- U1: Whether the reader needs real-time multi-user editing. Impact: This could change the relative value of local files. Next check: Ask the reader to rank collaboration as required, preferred, or unnecessary.
- U2: Whether a representative Notion export preserves links, attachments, and database structure well enough for migration. Impact: Poor fidelity could make migration cost unacceptable. Next check: Export and inspect one project.
