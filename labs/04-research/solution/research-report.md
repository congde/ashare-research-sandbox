# Obsidian vs Notion (personal knowledge work)

## Facts

- Obsidian publishes a personal-use license with optional paid Sync and Publish add-ons. Source: https://obsidian.md/pricing
- Notion publishes Free / Plus / Business tiers with documented page-history limits on Free. Source: https://www.notion.so/pricing
- Obsidian desktop stores notes as local Markdown files; mobile sync typically relies on Obsidian Sync or a self-managed folder sync setup. Source: https://help.obsidian.md/Obsidian/Obsidian+Sync
- Notion requires network access for most editing flows across desktop and mobile clients. Source: https://www.notion.so/help/category/mobile

## Inferences

- For a solo worker who treats offline access as a hard requirement, Obsidian
  aligns better with local-first storage.
- For a worker who prioritizes shared workspaces and lightweight databases inside
  notes, Notion aligns better—assuming network access is acceptable.

## Recommendations

- If offline access and file ownership matter most, pilot Obsidian for two weeks
  with a 20-note migration subset.
- If live collaboration and structured team pages matter most, stay on Notion
  and defer migration until requirements change.
- Do not decide on pricing alone; compare Sync/backup operational cost separately.

## Unknowns

- Whether the reader needs real-time multi-user editing (not specified in input).
- Whether company policy allows local-only storage for project notes.
